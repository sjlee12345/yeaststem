Abstract

Our STEM project is about how different carbohydrates change how much CO₂ yeast makes. In the project, we had used many different sugars to react with yeast, and found out what happens with those reactions, and get the result. Then, we had a comparison of CO₂ production. We had to find out which type of carbohydrate (e.g., glucose, sucrose, fructose, etc.) causes the yeast to produce the most or least CO₂ in a set time. This shows which sugars yeast can use most easily for respiration. After we had got all the results, we had made a graph of the results in a bar graph. The sugar that had produced most of the gas was dextrose, then fructose, glucose maltose, sucrose in that order. Before we started testing, we expected that the activity of glucose will be most active. After experiment, we can know our expecting was wrong, and dextrose makes most much CO₂

Hypothesis

Glucose will generate the most CO2. dextrose will be the next most, sucrose, fructose, maltose.

Independent Variables

Type of the carbohydrate(Glucose, fructose, sucrose, dextrose and maltose)

Dependent Variable

We will measure the amount of CO2 produced in cm3 by making the gas go through the delivery tube into the gas cylinder.

Control Variables

Name Value(Units) Method Explanation
Amount of carbohydrates 6g Measure with balance and weighing boat Keeping the amount of carbohydrates the same in different amounts can create different amounts of gas even with the same type.
Amount of yeast 3g Measure with a balance. Keeping the amount of yeast the same yeast the same as different amounts can create different amounts of gas even with the same carbohydrate.
Amount of water 100ml Measure with a measuring cylinder. Water changes the dilution which will change the amount of CO2 produced in a given time.
Duration of measurement 10 minutes Measure with a timer. Duration can change the amount of gas produced as this changes the time given for the yeast to digest.
The concentration of minerals in the water 0mg/cm³ Use distilled water. The amount of minerals can change the respiration rate due to changes in nutrition.
Water temperature 35ºC Use the water bath. Enough to activate yeast.
